Support with mental health concerns.

A service focusing on delivering interventions to support individuals who have mental health concerns.

We offer a safe and confidential space for you to talk and process your concerns. We prioritise validating your concerns as well as helping you identify coping strategies and other helpful ways of tackling your concerns.

Compassionate Care

Our service offers a range of targeted and specialized modalities for psychological difficulties, including stress, depression, PTSD, and anxiety disorders.

Neurodevelopmental Assessments

We screen for traits that align with Neurodevelopmental once we gather enough information to warrant further exploration into the diagnosis. We do utilise assessment tools such as ADOS-2, ADIR, CAT-Q, Coventry Grid and other assessment tools. These interventions are very useful in identifying traits that align with the diagnosis of Autistic Spectrum Condition.

Psychometric Tests

Interventions and assessment tools used vary from individual to individual. Tools will allow for thorough assessment and further exploration to determine if the diagnosis will be enough to describe the difficulties the young person presents with.

Hands Of Sympathy Programme:

1.
Support for families experiencing difficulties/challenges.
  • We work with families from all communities who do not acknowledge or struggle to validate people with mental health difficulties.
  • Working with families from the first-generation ethnic minority communities who are not fully conversant with the UK mental health services.
  • Families who lack insight into their mental health needs as they struggle to identify needs of the children under their care.
  • Giving insight into systemic issues that may impact the emotional wellbeing of young people.
  • Signposting them to other relevant services.
  • Offering emotional and practical support, raising parents’ confidence in mental health services.
  • Violence reduction.
2.
Support for young people.
  • 1:1 support for young people.
  • Support for young people at risk of school exclusion.
  • Emotional regulation.
  • Anger management program.
  • Stress management.
  • Family workshops.
3.
Our aim is to achieve the following outcomes for young people:
  • Increased confidence.
  • Increased self-esteem.
  • Emotional resilience.
  • Decision-making skills.
  • Insight into their difficulties and ways of maintaining psychological wellbeing.
4.
Our service criteria.
  • We are a tier two getting help service.
  • Inclusion criteria: Stress, Depression, Complex PTSD, PTSD stemming from childhood adverse conditions.
  • Various anxiety disorders: panic, social anxiety, OCD.
  • Low self-esteem.
  • Emotional regulation difficulties and difficulties in managing anger.
  • Ranging from 5 years upwards.

Hands of Sympathy is not a crisis service.

If you or anyone you know are in crisis, having thoughts of ending their life, or need immediate mental health support, here's what you can do:

  • Call the Samaritans 24hr helpline: 111 option 2
  • If you'd prefer not to call, text SHOUT to 85258
  • Present yourself to your nearest A&E.
